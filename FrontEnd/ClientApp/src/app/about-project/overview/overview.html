Overview

To keep imformed of local town or city government activities, attending regular Council meetings is the primary first step. It is here where bills are introduced, debated and ultimately voted on. Here is where new issues are introduced. Here formal reports from the department heads are read and discussed.

Current Access

But getting access to this information is not easy:

Perhaps some of have time to attend or watch all of two to three hour meetings But usually only a few minutes of each may be relevant to us.

Sometimes there are meeting minutes on government websites. But these are missing the important discusssions.

If this is a large city, perhaps there are transcripts available. But they are usually not user friendly.

Desired Access

Govmeeting will generate transcipts of government meetings, extract the information and populate a relational database. Then we will be able to do the following:

Browse information

  • Browse a meeting by issue, category, person speaking, related bill, etc.
  • Browse all topics discussed in the past year.
  • See what legislation was voted on and what the votes were.
  • Browse all public commentary on specific issues.
  • See which officials attended each of the past meetings.

Search for information

  • Search all past meetings for discussion on a specific issues.
  • Search for everthing that a specific official said on an issue.
  • Search for when specific bills were either discussed or acted upon.
  • Search for anything on a specific type of issue (public safety, zoning, etc)

Sign up for email or text alerts

  • when any specific issue is discussed at a meeting.
  • when a there is discussion in any category of issues (Recreation, Zoning, Animal Control, etc).
  • when any new legislature is proposed.
  • whenever any search criteria that you set up returns results.
  • You can also automatically receive a summary of each meeting.

Use graphical and statistical tools

  • Graphically see the types of issue discussed over time.
  • Graphically see the amount of time spent on issue, bills, etc.
  • Compare issues for number of alerts on them, etc.
  • Compare attendence and involvement of elected officials

Comparisons with other communities

  • Compare types of issues that are discussed across different communities.
  • Compare how similar issues are handled.
  • Compare meeting structures and time allotment.
  • Compare procedures for public comment.
